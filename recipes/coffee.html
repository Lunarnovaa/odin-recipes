How to Brew Conventional Coffee

You'll love this beverage for early mornings or mid-day productivity boosts.

Look. Lattes are great. But there's a certain feeling standard coffee will give you that nothing can quite match. I know that, and you know that. So here's my brewing recipe for coffee, too.

This one's gonna be a little less in depth than the other one. Also I don't steam my milk for standard coffee--- it's kinda supposed to be a lower effort thing, plus it doesn't matter as much because the milk affects the temperature less.

Anyways, let's get into it. Get your nostrils ready for that perfect coffee smell :)

Things you'll need:

  • A coffee pot (I'll be supplying instructions for a Moka Pot, as that's what I have.)
  • Home-ground or pre-bought coffee grinds
  • Measuring cups
  • Milk

Steps:

  1. Measure out 2 cups of water.
      2. This will be roughly how much coffee you'll be making. If 2 cups is too much, you can use less--- just be sure to preserve the ratios between water and coffee grounds.
  2. Measure out 2 tablespoons of coffee grounds.
  3. In the bottom section of the Moka Pot, pour your water in.
  4. In the middle funnel-like section, pour your coffee grounds in.
    • Be careful not to spill any!
    • Additionally, be sure not to have the pile be "heaping." It should be flush.
  5. Screw it all together and place in on a burner roughly the size of the base of the Moka Pot.
  6. Once the Moka Pot is steaming (should take around 8-10 minutes), take it away from heat and stir the contents.
    • This is because the coffee brewed at the start is stronger than the stuff brewed near the end.
  7. Pour the coffee in a mug, and add how much milk you want.
    • Remember you can always add more, but you can't take away :)
  8. Add sugar or syrup to it if you want!
    • I personally like to add vanilla syrup. You can buy it at the store.
