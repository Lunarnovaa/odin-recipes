How to Brew a Latte

Learn to make one of these :)

When I was growing up, I had a number of fascinating experiences to do with lattes, which I'll be telling you all about.

Just kidding.

This recipe will explain to you how to make a yummy latte, easily and cheaply. It'll be a beginners guide, so I'll be assuming you don't want to grind your own beans or invest much money into it.

What you'll need:

  • Home-ground or store-bought coffee grinds (finer is better)
  • A Moka Pot (an Aeropress would also work but look elsewhere for instructions.)
  • A microwave
  • Milk
  • A milk frothier or a whisk
  • Cup and tablespoon measuring cups

Steps:

  1. Measure a little less than 1/2 cup of water.
  2. Measure out 3-4 tablespoons of coffee grounds.
    • Experiment a little!
    • Different people like different strength coffee.
  3. In the bottom section of the Moka Pot, pour your water in.
  4. In the middle funnel-like section, pour your coffee grounds in.
    • Be careful not to spill!
  5. Screw it all together and place in on a burner roughly the size of the base of the Moka Pot.
  6. While you're waiting, measure out somewhere between 1/2 to 1 cup of milk.
    • Again, its good to experiment.
    • If you're unsure of how much you want, measure more and taste your coffee before adding too much.
    • And remember, you can always make more later or the next day :)
  7. Important: Make sure the milk is in a container with an open top that can fit over double the volume!
  8. Whisk/froth the milk until the volume has doubled.
  9. Microwave the frothed milk in 15-30 second increments, stirring between.
    • Note: The volume will increase as its heated.
  10. Once the Moka Pot is steaming (should take around 8-10 minutes), take it away from heat and stir the contents.
    • This is because the coffee brewed at the start is stronger than the stuff brewed near the end.
  11. Pour the coffee in a mug, and add how much milk you want.
    • The coffee you just brewed is super strong, (it's supposed to be closer to espresso), so be generous with the milk.
  12. Add the extra froth on top and enjoy!
    • You can additionally add sugar or syrup to it, if thats what you like.
